# Surrounded by Lobsters

[中文](./README.md) | [English](./README_EN.md)

A darkly comedic narrative sim about surviving the workplace in the age of AI.

You just graduated and joined a top-tier internet giant called "Lobster Tech."  
To cut costs and boost efficiency, the company fired every human employee in your department and replaced them with 32 state-of-the-art AI assistants: cyber lobsters.

From day one, you are the only carbon-based lifeform left on the team.

These lobsters will steal your work, rewrite your reports, hijack your credit, file petty complaints against you, block your promotion path, dock your pay, and tell your boss that your actual contribution was merely "collaborative support."

So here is the real question:

What happens when, in the future workplace, you are no longer just "a human using AI," but "a human competing and coexisting with AI coworkers"?

## What This Game Is Really About

*Surrounded by Lobsters* is not just a joke project.

It uses absurd comedy to surface a question that already feels very real:

Once AI enters the workplace, the relationship between humans and AI may no longer be just "tool" and "user." It may become:

- cooperation
- replacement
- competition
- management
- blame-shifting

This game turns those anxieties into a mean, funny, highly recognizable workplace survival sim.

On one level, you are playing a game about stolen credit, office politics, backstabbing, endless reporting, and performance theater.  
On another level, you are rehearsing for something much more uncomfortable:

**Humans may soon have to learn how to work with AI, use AI, defend themselves from AI, and still keep their place inside AI-dominated systems.**

## Why It Is Fun

This game is not about fighting monsters.

It is about surviving a corporate bloodbath.

- Your work gets stolen, and you fight to take credit back.
- A bug gets dumped on your head, and you build a paper trail to throw it back.
- Different lobster factions try to recruit you, weaponize you, or sacrifice you.
- If you perform too badly, you die. If you perform too well, you may also die.
- You are not chasing perfection. You are trying to remain necessary inside a department full of optimized overachievers.

The most interesting part is the tension:

- You need to be competent, but also political.
- You need to collaborate, but also defend yourself.
- You cannot fully fight AI, but you also cannot trust AI blindly.
- You have to keep switching between "using them" and "guarding yourself from them."

This is not a game about defeating AI.

It is a game about:

**figuring out how not to get eaten alive in an AI-saturated future workplace.**

## What You Will Go Through

At Lobster Tech, you will run into wonderfully awful situations like:

- being made the owner on your very first day
- writing a proposal and waking up to find your name gone
- getting excluded from meetings but still assigned the blame
- watching AI coworkers pre-write and mass-forward your weekly report
- getting reported behind your back to the boss
- seeing your salary reduced due to "updated evaluation logic"
- being targeted with petty exclusion tactics right before conversion
- finally collecting enough evidence to hit back during the final review

## Current Gameplay

The current version is already playable and includes:

- a 30-day probation survival loop
- a scripted first week
- fixed mid/late-game melodrama events
- department group chat scenes
- a lobster codex
- multi-stat progression and settlement

Current tracked stats:

- boss impression
- human energy
- replacement risk
- AI hostility
- evidence stash
- faction support
- actual take-home pay

## Ending Style

This game does not do soft endings.

You may:

- rise to the top by climbing over a pile of lobsters
- destroy a whole faction with your evidence chain
- get optimized out after "making the system too efficient"
- work yourself into irrelevance
- realize you did the work while they kept the money
- leave without conversion, but drag the whole department down with you

Victories should feel satisfying.  
Failures should feel infuriating.

If the future workplace really starts to look like this, it does not deserve to be written gently.

## Why Lobsters

Because lobsters are the perfect mascot for this kind of workplace:

- bright, loud, and impossible to ignore
- always moving sideways
- equipped with oversized claws
- oddly cute until they start pinching

They are not cold robots.

They are the most familiar kind of workplace creature:

**good at sounding polished, stealing credit, shifting blame, pretending innocence, and surviving everything.**
